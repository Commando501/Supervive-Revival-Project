INTERPRETATION — the remaining blocker
---------------------------------------
"Invalid property terminator handle - Handle=22" is a RepLayout HANDLE-space
mismatch (finer-grained than the field-index space that N=0 lived in). The
field mapping now aligns (no more "Invalid replicated field"), but the flattened
property-cmd HANDLE stream desyncs: the client reads handle 22 where it expects
the block terminator (handle 0). The bits within ServerState desynced the reader.

Cause: our ServerState wire representation (member-wise int32 State + int32
Version = 2 RepLayout cmds, 64 bits) does NOT bit-match the client's
PoolableActorServerState. Candidates for the mismatch:
  - State is an EnumProperty<UInt32Property> on the client; RepLayout/enum net
    serialization may use a BIT-PACKED width (< 32 bits) rather than full 32,
    so our 32-bit int32 over-reads and desyncs everything after it.
  - PoolableActorServerState may have a custom NetSerialize (WithNetSerializer)
    => a SINGLE opaque cmd, not 2 member cmds. Then handle counts differ.
All stock structs (RepMovement/RepAttachment/Rotator/Vector) expand identically
on both sides, so ServerState is the sole novel expansion and the prime suspect.
